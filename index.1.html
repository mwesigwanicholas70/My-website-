🔢 1. Mechanics & Motion

Kinematics (Motion with Constant Acceleration)

Q1: A car accelerates uniformly from rest to 30 m/s in 10 seconds. Calculate: (a) acceleration (b) distance covered
✓ Answer:

(a) Acceleration:

a = (v - u) / t = (30 - 0) / 10 = 3 m/s²

(b) Distance covered:

s = ut + ½at² = 0 + ½(3)(10)² = 150 m

Or using: v² = u² + 2as → 30² = 0 + 2(3)s → s = 150 m

Q2: An object is thrown vertically upward with initial velocity 20 m/s. Find: (a) time to reach maximum height (b) maximum height (use g = 10 m/s²)
✓ Answer:

(a) Time to reach maximum height:

At max height, v = 0

v = u - gt → 0 = 20 - 10t → t = 2 seconds

(b) Maximum height:

h = ut - ½gt² = 20(2) - ½(10)(2)² = 40 - 20 = 20 m

Forces & Newton's Laws

Q3: A force of 50 N is applied to a 10 kg mass on a frictionless surface. What is the acceleration?
✓ Answer:

Using Newton's Second Law: F = ma

a = F / m = 50 / 10 = 5 m/s²
Q4: Three forces act on an object: 10 N (East), 6 N (West), and 8 N (North). Find the resultant force and its direction.
✓ Answer:

Horizontal component: 10 - 6 = 4 N (East)

Vertical component: 8 N (North)

Resultant = √(4² + 8²) = √(16 + 64) = √80 = 8.94 N
Direction: tan θ = 8/4 = 2 → θ = 63.4° North of East

Energy, Work & Power

Q5: A 2 kg object is lifted 5 meters vertically. Calculate the work done (g = 10 m/s²)
✓ Answer:

Work = Force × Distance (in direction of force)

W = mgh = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 J
Q6: A machine does 5000 J of work in 25 seconds. What is its power?
✓ Answer:
P = W / t = 5000 / 25 = 200 W
Q7: A car of mass 1000 kg has a kinetic energy of 500,000 J. Find its velocity.
✓ Answer:
KE = ½mv² → 500,000 = ½(1000)v²
v² = 1000 → v = 31.6 m/s

⚡ 2. Electricity & Magnetism

Current, Voltage & Resistance

Q8: A 12 V battery supplies 3 A of current to a resistor. Calculate the resistance.
✓ Answer:

Using Ohm's Law: V = IR

R = V / I = 12 / 3 = 4 Ω
Q9: Three resistors of 2 Ω, 3 Ω, and 5 Ω are connected in series. Find total resistance.
✓ Answer:

For series: Total Resistance = R₁ + R₂ + R₃

R_total = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 Ω
Q10: Two resistors of 6 Ω and 3 Ω are connected in parallel. Find total resistance.
✓ Answer:

For parallel: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂

1/R_total = 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/6 + 2/6 = 3/6
R_total = 2 Ω

Electrical Power & Energy

Q11: An electric bulb rated 100 W at 200 V is used for 5 hours. Calculate energy consumed in kWh.
✓ Answer:
Energy = Power × Time = 100 W × 5 h = 500 Wh = 0.5 kWh
Q12: Calculate the current in a 1500 W heater when connected to a 250 V supply.
✓ Answer:
P = VI → I = P / V = 1500 / 250 = 6 A

Magnetism

Q13: What is the direction of the magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field?
✓ Answer:

Fleming's Left-Hand Rule:

• Thumb = direction of motion/force

• First finger = direction of magnetic Field (N to S)

• Second finger = direction of Current

The force is perpendicular to both the current and magnetic field.

🌊 3. Waves & Sound

Wave Properties

Q14: A wave has a wavelength of 4 m and frequency of 5 Hz. Calculate its speed.
✓ Answer:
v = f × λ = 5 × 4 = 20 m/s
Q15: Sound travels at 340 m/s. If the wavelength is 0.85 m, find the frequency.
✓ Answer:
f = v / λ = 340 / 0.85 = 400 Hz

Sound & Acoustics

Q16: What is an echo? Explain how echo is used to measure distance.
✓ Answer:

Echo: A sound wave that reflects from a surface and returns to the source.

Distance measurement:

• Send a sound signal toward an object

• Measure time (t) for echo to return

• Distance to object = (v × t) / 2

• We divide by 2 because sound travels to object and back

Example: If echo returns in 2 seconds at 340 m/s:

Distance = (340 × 2) / 2 = 340 m

💡 4. Light & Optics

Reflection & Refraction

Q17: State the laws of reflection of light.
✓ Answer:

Laws of Reflection:

1. The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection

2. The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane

3. The angles are measured from the normal (perpendicular to surface)

Q18: Light travels from air (n=1) into water (n=1.33) at an angle of 45°. Calculate the angle of refraction.
✓ Answer:

Using Snell's Law: n₁sin θ₁ = n₂sin θ₂

1 × sin(45°) = 1.33 × sin(θ₂)
0.707 = 1.33 × sin(θ₂)
sin(θ₂) = 0.531 → θ₂ = 32.1°

Lenses & Mirrors

Q19: A concave mirror has a focal length of 10 cm. An object is placed 30 cm from the mirror. Find the image distance and magnification.
✓ Answer:

Using mirror formula: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v

1/10 = 1/30 + 1/v
1/v = 1/10 - 1/30 = 3/30 - 1/30 = 2/30
v = 15 cm (real, inverted image)

Magnification:

m = -v/u = -15/30 = -0.5

Image is 0.5 times the size of object and inverted

Q20: What is the power of a convex lens with focal length 50 cm?
✓ Answer:
Power = 1/f (in meters)
P = 1/0.5 = +2 Diopters (D)

🔥 5. Heat & Thermodynamics

Temperature & Heat

Q21: Convert 25°C to Kelvin and Fahrenheit.
✓ Answer:

To Kelvin:

K = °C + 273 = 25 + 273 = 298 K

To Fahrenheit:

°F = (9/5 × °C) + 32 = (9/5 × 25) + 32 = 45 + 32 = 77°F
Q22: How much heat is required to raise the temperature of 2 kg of water by 20°C? (Specific heat capacity of water = 4200 J/kg°C)
✓ Answer:
Q = mcΔT = 2 × 4200 × 20 = 168,000 J = 168 kJ

Expansion & States of Matter

Q23: A metal rod of length 1 m at 20°C is heated to 120°C. If the linear expansion coefficient is 12×10⁻⁶ /°C, find the new length.
✓ Answer:
ΔL = αL₀ΔT = 12×10⁻⁶ × 1 × (120-20)
ΔL = 12×10⁻⁶ × 100 = 1.2×10⁻³ m = 0.0012 m
New length = 1 + 0.0012 = 1.0012 m

Thermodynamics Laws

Q24: State the First Law of Thermodynamics.
✓ Answer:

First Law of Thermodynamics:

"The change in internal energy of a system equals the heat added to the system minus the work done by the system."

ΔU = Q - W

Where:

• ΔU = change in internal energy

• Q = heat added to the system

• W = work done by the system

⚛️ 6. Atomic Physics

Atomic Structure

Q25: Describe the structure of an atom according to Bohr's model.
✓ Answer:

Bohr's Atomic Model:

• The nucleus contains protons and neutrons at the center

• Electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed circular orbits (shells/energy levels)

• Each shell can hold a maximum number of electrons

• Electrons jump between orbits by absorbing or emitting energy

• The innermost shell (K shell) can hold max 2 electrons

• The next shell (L shell) can hold max 8 electrons

• The outermost shell determines chemical properties

Q26: What is the difference between atomic number and mass number?
✓ Answer:

Atomic Number (Z):

• Number of protons in the nucleus

• Determines the element's identity

• Determines number of electrons (in neutral atom)

Mass Number (A):

• Total number of protons + neutrons

• Determines the isotope

Number of neutrons = A - Z

Example: Carbon-12 (¹²C)

• Atomic number = 6 (6 protons)

• Mass number = 12 (6 protons + 6 neutrons)

Radioactivity

Q27: What are alpha, beta, and gamma radiations? What are they composed of?
✓ Answer:

Alpha (α) Radiation:

• Composed of Helium-4 nuclei (2 protons + 2 neutrons)

• Least penetrating (stopped by paper)

• Positive charge

Beta (β) Radiation:

• Composed of electrons (from neutron decay)

• More penetrating than alpha (need aluminum foil)

• Negative charge

Gamma (γ) Radiation:

• Electromagnetic waves (high energy photons)

• Most penetrating (need lead/concrete)

• No charge

Q28: A radioactive sample has a half-life of 5 years. If initial mass is 100 g, how much remains after 15 years?
✓ Answer:

Number of half-lives in 15 years = 15 / 5 = 3 half-lives

After 1st half-life (5 yrs): 100 → 50 g
After 2nd half-life (10 yrs): 50 → 25 g
After 3rd half-life (15 yrs): 25 → 12.5 g

Or: N = N₀ × (1/2)ⁿ = 100 × (1/2)³ = 100 × 1/8 = 12.5 g
